## Research

I work on the problem of building AI systems that remain reliably aligned under adversarial conditions and distributional shift. My work sits at the intersection of alignment science, mechanistic interpretability, and AI governance — with a particular focus on what breaks alignment and why.

Current active directions:

**Adversarial self-play in alignment** — developing minimax training frameworks that expose and reduce sycophantic behavior in large language models without degrading capability. Core paper: *Breaking the Mirror* (AdverSplay-GRPO, targeting COLM 2026).

**Belief geometry in deep RL** — collaborating with Prof. Fernando Rosas (University of Sussex) through the MARS 4.0 program at the Cambridge AI Safety Hub on how belief-state representations form and degrade in reinforcement learning agents.

**Structured ignorance in AI systems** — investigating how models can be made to reliably represent the boundaries of their own competence, targeting EIML @ ICML 2026.

**AI governance and autonomous systems** — examining alignment failures that emerge from institutional and organizational structures rather than model internals.
